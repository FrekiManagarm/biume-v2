# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: June 29, 2026*

## Scope

This document is the marketing source of truth for Biume.

Current strategic scope: **France only**.

Primary market: independent osteopathes animaliers and animal therapists who need to make each session clearer, more professional, and easier to follow for animal owners.

Important assumption: this is a working V1 based on the repository, current public competitor messaging, and strategic analysis. It should be updated after 10-20 practitioner interviews.

## Product Overview

**One-liner:**
Biume helps animal therapists turn every session into a clear owner-facing follow-up that builds trust and brings clients back.

**What it does:**
Biume creates professional post-session summaries, owner-friendly explanations, follow-up reminders, and longitudinal animal timelines from practitioner observations. The product should not position itself as an autonomous diagnostic or veterinary decision tool; it should position itself as a practitioner-controlled communication and follow-up system.

**Product category:**
Post-session follow-up and owner communication software for animal therapists.

Secondary category:
AI-assisted reporting for osteopathes animaliers.

Avoid leading with:
- Practice management software
- Veterinary AI scribe
- Animal health record app
- Diagnostic AI

**Product type:**
Vertical SaaS for animal care professionals.

**Business model:**
Self-serve SaaS with a short free trial, plus early concierge onboarding for pilot practitioners.

**Current public pricing in the repo:**
- 29.99 EUR/month monthly
- 24.99 EUR/month annual billing
- 15-day free trial
- No card required

## Target Audience

**Target professionals:**
- Osteopathes animaliers
- Animal therapists
- Equine and canine bodywork practitioners
- Practitioners doing recurring care where owner trust and follow-up matter

**Geography:**
France first.

**Business profile:**
- Independent practitioner or very small practice
- Often mobile or hybrid mobile/cabinet
- Works directly with owners
- Relies on word of mouth, repeat sessions, reputation, and professional presentation

**Decision-maker:**
The practitioner.

**End beneficiary:**
The owner, who needs to understand what happened during the session, what to observe, and when follow-up is useful.

**Primary use case:**
After a session, the practitioner wants to send a clear, professional, reassuring summary that the owner can understand and act on.

**Jobs to be done:**
- Make the session feel more valuable after the owner has left.
- Reduce repeated explanations and vague follow-up messages.
- Encourage appropriate follow-up and repeat appointments.
- Build a professional record of comfort, mobility, observations, and owner feedback over time.

**Use cases:**
- First session summary for a new animal
- Follow-up after osteopathy or manual therapy session
- Owner-friendly post-session instructions
- J+7 check-in after a session
- J+30 rebooking prompt when follow-up is relevant
- Before/after timeline for recurring cases

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem:**
The value of an animal therapy session is often clear in the room but fades afterward because the owner does not fully remember what was observed, why it matters, what to monitor, and when to book a follow-up.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Practice management tools help with agenda, clients, invoices, and sometimes reports, but they do not strongly own the post-session trust and rebooking loop.
- Veterinary AI scribes focus on clinical notes, transcription, SOAP-style structure, and clinic workflows, not independent animal therapists.
- Owner apps help owners centralize animal information, but they are not built around the practitioner's session narrative and follow-up process.
- Manual PDFs or messages are flexible, but inconsistent and time-consuming.

**What it costs practitioners:**
- Lost time writing reports or messages
- Lower perceived value after the session
- Missed follow-up opportunities
- Weaker differentiation from other practitioners
- Less structured long-term memory of the animal's evolution

**Emotional tension:**
"I know my work has value, but I need the owner to understand it, remember it, and come back at the right moment."

## Competitive Landscape - France Only

### Direct Competitors

**Animalib**
- Positioning: management software for osteopathes animaliers.
- Strengths: clear vertical focus, free functional plan, agenda, clients, consultations, invoicing, reports, mobile/web, offline, tours.
- Why Biume should not fight head-on: Animalib already owns a strong "manage my practice" promise with a free entry point.
- Biume angle: not "manage your activity"; "make owners understand the session and come back."

**Hunimalis**
- Positioning: all-in-one management software for animal professionals, including osteopathes animaliers.
- Strengths: appointments, consultations, records, billing, online visibility, French support, free entry.
- Why Biume should not fight head-on: broad management suite and established operational tooling.
- Biume angle: specialized post-session owner communication and follow-up.

**Stenko**
- Positioning: app for animal professionals and owners.
- Strengths: professional and owner sides, planning, online booking, animal records, reports, invoicing, payment, owner access, beta/free entry.
- Why it matters most: Stenko is closest to the owner relationship and can credibly move into follow-up.
- Biume angle: deeper practitioner-controlled session narrative, progress loop, and therapy-specific longitudinal memory.

**MyTour**
- Positioning: admin, tours, and business management for mobile animal professionals.
- Strengths: route/tour planning, invoicing, mileage, mobile workflow.
- Why Biume should not fight head-on: MyTour is an operational/admin product.
- Biume angle: after-care communication and repeat-session loop.

### Secondary Competitors

**NeoVoice**
- Positioning: owner-side animal health and wellbeing follow-up app, with carnet, reminders, documents, appointments, specialist directory, and report archiving.
- Strengths: owner-first surface, health/wellbeing record, partnership credibility in the animal osteopathy ecosystem.
- Why it matters: NeoVoice can own "animal follow-up" in the owner's mind.
- Biume angle: Biume should not try to become the generic owner health record. It should be the practitioner's post-session follow-up layer: the summary, instructions, check-in, and rebooking loop generated from a real session.

**MyPawScribe and French veterinary AI tools**
- Positioning: AI-assisted veterinary reports and consultation documentation.
- Strengths: clear AI documentation value, veterinary workflow, transcription/reporting.
- Why Biume should not fight head-on: veterinary workflows and buyer expectations differ from animal therapists.
- Biume angle: not a veterinary scribe; a therapy-specific owner communication and follow-up system.

### Indirect Competitors

**Manual workflow**
- Notes app, Google Docs, Canva, email, WhatsApp, printed PDFs.
- Strength: free and flexible.
- Weakness: inconsistent, hard to scale, no structured owner feedback, no rebooking loop.

**Generic CRM / booking tools**
- Strength: scheduling and reminders.
- Weakness: not therapy-specific and not built around animal progress.

## Differentiation

**Primary wedge:**
Biume transforms each session into a professional owner follow-up that makes progress visible and encourages the next right action.

**Key differentiators:**
- Owner-facing summary controlled by the practitioner
- Progress timeline by animal
- J+7 and J+30 follow-up loops
- Structured observations around comfort, mobility, zones, laterality, severity, and recommendations
- Designed specifically for osteopathes animaliers and animal therapists in France
- ROI story tied to rebooking and retention, not admin replacement

**How we do it differently:**
Biume starts from the post-session moment rather than from the admin back office. The practitioner enters or validates observations, Biume turns them into an owner-friendly follow-up, and the system captures owner feedback over time.

**Why that is better:**
It creates immediate perceived value for the owner, reduces repetitive communication for the practitioner, and gradually builds a longitudinal memory that competitors cannot copy with a simple PDF template.

**Why customers choose Biume:**
Because they want every session to look more professional, be better understood, and create a stronger reason for owners to continue care.

## Pricing Strategy

### Pricing Thesis

Biume should not price against free admin tools.
Biume should price against the economic value of one recovered follow-up appointment.

Core pricing message:
`Un seul rendez-vous repris peut rembourser Biume.`

### Current Recommendation

Keep the simple public price for now:
- Monthly: 29.99 EUR/month
- Annual: 24.99 EUR/month billed annually
- Trial: 15 days, no card required

Rationale:
- Simple and understandable for independent practitioners.
- Low enough for self-serve testing.
- High enough to avoid being perceived as a generic free admin utility.
- Defensible if tied to rebooking and owner follow-up.

### What Not To Do Yet

- Do not launch a broad free plan. France has strong free/freemium competitors for admin, and a free plan would anchor Biume against them.
- Do not create many tiers before value is validated.
- Do not charge per owner or per animal initially; it may create friction exactly where Biume needs usage.
- Do not sell "AI credits" as the value metric. Practitioners do not buy tokens; they buy professional follow-up and repeat business.

### Pilot Offer

Use a concierge pilot before optimizing pricing:

**Offer:**
10 premium owner follow-ups generated from real practitioner notes.

**Target:**
10-20 French osteopathes animaliers.

**Ask:**
They send real notes from 3 sessions each.

**What Biume produces:**
- Owner summary
- Points observed
- Practical post-session notes
- J+7 check-in text
- Suggested next-step/rebooking prompt

**Validation questions:**
- Would you send this as-is to an owner?
- Would this make you look more professional?
- Would this save you time?
- Would it help a client book the next session?
- Would you pay 29.99 EUR/month for this?
